Failure of my startup journey

2017-06-17
It is quite sad when I was writing up this blog post because I am trying to reference down the key factors of why my startup to be failure.


Is been almost 1.5 years since we have created our startup and there is a lot of up and down moment and recently we have decided to walk away. Of course the major reason as always is about money. The initial seed funding which my business partner and I have put together ourselves has finished already and so far we still can’t secure any further funding or start getting any traction.


Second key issue is our network. We initially have three key contacts inside the industry which help us kicking off in the early stage. Unfortunately, two of them have left the company and the final company also got acquired by other one( good for them). And due to the confidential rule or whatever, they can’t really tell us all those factors even thought they already resigned when we start approaching them about our startup. This create a lot of impact to our journey particularly a lot of potential client is looking at them as their guidance.


Third issue is related to our decision making. We are too concern about our equity and thinking too much about losing too much equity in the early stage. We believe we know very well about how to build our product and we don’t need any extra help until a later stage so we don’t have to lose equity when people only input a small amount of effort. Unfortunately, a big name is exactly what we are missing in our startup. If we willing to use equity to get some big guns on board, I reckon this will become a different story. At the end of the day, this is just our first startup, we should focus on getting our product right. Once we become famous ourselves, we will have more bargaining powers in the market.

Venture start

2015-05-07
Today is a remarkable day which is the day that I decided to start my startup ventures with another business partner.


My business partner and I were working in the same company before which is a sass platform for retail after sales services. During that period, we discovered that the product content is a mess in different system without any standardisation. For example: retailer A like to call mobile as handset in their database and retailer B like to call it mobilephone. So this create a lot of hassle to vendors especially when they try to submit the product content to retailer. Not to mention the process of uploading product content from vendors to retailers is still remaining in stone ages with emails and phone calls all the time. Surprisingly, people still try to overcome this issue manually and our startup is trying to streamline the whole process by create a standardized platform to hold the product content and feed to anyone who want it.


We have already spent a few months for R&D and we have received a lot of positive feedback so far and people are saying they are looking forward to see our product. This give us a lot of positive energy and confident to start building our product.


We have chosen Laravel 4 to build our product purely because I familiar with it the most and laravel 5 hasn’t come out at this time. We didn’t really spend a lot of time to puzzling our tech stack since we want to rush out our first prototype ASAP to test out the market and also try to collect more valuable feedback from potential customers.
